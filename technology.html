World War II changed the technological world that we live in now. Without the war, we would not have much of the technology we have today. From Jet engines to Jerry Cans, the origin of this everyday technology can all be traced back to World War 2.

Penicillin

In 1943 the US War Production Board came up with a plan for the mass distribution of penicillin to Allied troops fighting in Europe. By 1944, the US had produced 2.3 million doses and the medicine is credited with saving an estimated 12 to 15 per cent of casualties.

Nuclear Energy

The potential power stored within the Atom had been known for a long time, but it was during WWII that the race to harness its power really began. The US got there first with the Manhattan Project, which developed the first Atom bomb in history. On August 6th 1945 the bomb was dropped on Hiroshima, then on August 9th 1945 a second bomb was dropped on Nagasaki. It's estimated that between 150,000 and 244,000 people were killed as a direct result.

Synthetics

A shortage of oil meant that the Germans desperately needed an alternative and came up with a blend of adipic acid ester with poly(ethylene) oil. It helped keep the Luftwaffe in the air during the duration of the war. The Americans also developed their own synthetic oil and started using it in the airforce once it was discovered that synthetic oils made the planes easier to start in winter and reduced soot deposits in oil radiators.
